How It Works

Simple workflow for dealers and direct buyers

Champion Auto Finance helps structure, submit, and fund deals without hidden markups or confusing lender handoffs.

For Dealership Partners

Who this is for: Independent dealerships or sellers who prefer to outsource financing or do not maintain a direct bank.

  1. Dealer submits the customer application and vehicle details.
  2. Champion matches the deal to the best lender fit.
  3. Approvals, stipulations, and deal structure are reviewed.
  4. Contracting and compliance support are coordinated.
  5. Dealer completes the sale while funding is finalized.
  • One point of access to multiple lenders
  • No rate packing, true lender buy rates
  • Full compliance and documentation support

For Individual Customers

Who this is for: Consumers financing a lease buyout, private party purchase, or marketplace vehicle.

  1. Customer completes a secure application.
  2. Champion reviews income, structure, and vehicle profile.
  3. Lender options are sourced based on the transaction.
  4. Terms are reviewed clearly with no hidden markups.
  5. Champion coordinates with the seller or title holder to fund the purchase.
  • No rate packing, true lender buy rates
  • Support through title, payoff, and funding steps
  • Professional guidance from application to completion

FAQs

Common questions

Do you work with independent dealerships?

Yes. The current positioning and workflow are built specifically around independent dealers that want a strong financing partner and a cleaner process from submission through funding.

Do you only handle prime credit?

No. The financing model is intended to support multiple credit tiers, including prime, near-prime, and subprime scenarios depending on lender fit and deal structure.

Do you mark up lender approvals?

The public-facing message is the opposite: Champion emphasizes true lender buy rates and no rate packing.

Can consumers work directly with Champion?

Yes. The current site messaging includes support for lease buyouts, private-party purchases, and marketplace transactions in addition to dealership-originated deals.
